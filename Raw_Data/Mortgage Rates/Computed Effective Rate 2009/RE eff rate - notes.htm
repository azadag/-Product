I calc’d this based on the 10 yr term and did not come up with much difference. See below and attached for notes and methodology

 

NOTES/METHODOLOGY          

Step 1   Compute PMT based on:

            -the average ARM rate from Freddie Mac 1yr ARM for the 3 months in the quarter

            -$100,000 original loan amount

            -30 yr loan term

            does not account for adjustments in the rate for this calculation

Step 2   Need to determine the outstanding balance after the 10 years according to FHFB methodology

            From: http://www.fhfb.gov/Default.aspx?Page=52  "The effective interest rate includes the amortization of initial fees and charges over a 10-year   period, which is the historical assumption of the average life of a mortgage loan."

            using the pmt computed in step 1

            Term is 360 minus 120 (or 10 years) which is the remaining loan term.

            Compute PV

Step 3   Solve for the effective interest rate based on

            -Pmt from step 1

            -PV is equal to the original loan amount less the points charged

            -Points are equal to the average of the 3 months in the quarter based on Freddie Mac 1YR ARM points

            Compute RATE to get the effective rate.
